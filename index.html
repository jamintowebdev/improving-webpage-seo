Meta description

The next meta tag is the description.
It acts like a synopsis on the back of a book. The title is the heading, and the
meta description is an extra couple sentences of information that further
describe what the content is about.
The description is created using a <meta> tag:

<meta name="description" content="Join Now to learn the basics or advance your existing skills.">

Headings

We have already seen headings in the previous modules.
They are created using the tags <h1> to <h6> and help organize your content.

In addition to organizing the content, headings also help with your SEO.
Search engines use them to determine segments of content on your page.

Here is the hierarchy of heading tags:
<h1> - usually reserved for web page titles.
<h2> - highlights the topic of the title.
<h3> - reflects points in regards to the topic.
<h4> - supports points from <h3> .
<h5> and <h6> - not often used, but can be used to further breakdown points of <h4> .

Images

Images play an important role in your web pages.

According to statistics, articles that include images get about 94% more views than articles without them. Images are created using the <img> tag and use the <src> attribute to refer to the image file.

Links

Links play a vital part in SEO. They determine the ranking of your website on
search engines and act as a "vote" to your page.
Having many quality links to your page increases the page ranking.

The same applies to outbound links.
Linking out to something that isn't relevant to your topic can result in search
penalties.

If you need to link to a page but don't want search engines to consider that
connection, you can use "nofollow" links.
By doing this, you are preventing the external domain to which you are linking
from benefiting from additional domain authority.

Here is how to create a "nofollow" link:

<a href="https://somesite.com/" rel="nofollow">

Open Graph Tags

There are special tags that are used by social media.
These tags specify how your page should look when shared on social
networks such as Facebook.

Facebook, for instance, uses Open Graph tags to display information when
your content is shared.

Open Graph tags are meta tags that go into the <head> of your page. The most
popular ones are title, description and image.

Here's an example of a open graph tags:

<meta property="og:title" content="SoloLearn: Learn to Code">

<meta property="og:description" content="Join Now to learn the basics or advance your existing skills">

<meta property="og:image" content="logo.png">


Note, that the tags use the prefix og: before their names.
